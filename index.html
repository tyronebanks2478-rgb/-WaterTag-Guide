Overview

What is WaterTag?

WaterTag is an outdoor water battle game made for organized group activity. Instead of players simply spraying water randomly, the field includes clear rules, team zones, refill barrels, objective play, and water-responsive vests that make hits visible and meaningful.

💦

Water-based action

The game keeps the fun of water play, but adds rules and missions so the activity feels like a real event experience.

🎯

Objective-driven rounds

Teams can play around flags, refill bases, scoring zones, and field control instead of only trying to soak each other.

🚚

Mobile attraction format

The attraction can be set up at camps, resorts, school fields, outdoor venues, private events, and activity days.

How it works

The barrels are the battlefield.

In WaterTag, barrels are not only water storage. They create the flow of the game. Players refill, defend, attack, move between zones, and make tactical choices around each barrel.

  • Refill barrels serve as bases, objectives, cover, and scoring points.
  • Water-responsive vests make the game clearer and reduce guessing.
  • Short, high-energy sessions make it easier to rotate large groups.
  • Optional scoring tools help operators deliver a more professional recap.
Simple to explain

Why people understand it fast

A strong attraction should not need a long explanation. WaterTag is easy to understand because everyone already knows the fun of water play. The difference is that Falcon turns that familiar idea into a team mission.

  • Put on the vest: Players wear water-responsive vests that help make hits visible.
  • Choose teams: Groups split into teams and play against each other in short rounds.
  • Refill and move: Barrels create bases and movement points across the field.
  • Play the mission: The team goal can change depending on the game mode.
Game modes

Six ways to play WaterTag

WaterTag is stronger than a normal water game because it gives operators different session formats. That keeps the activity fresh for campers, guests, students, employees, and repeat groups.

1

Team Deathmatch

Fast team competition where players score through hits, pressure, and field control.

2

Capture the Flag

A mission format where teams cross the field, protect water resources, and complete the objective.

3

Battle Royale

A high-energy format built around survival, movement, and last-team-standing gameplay.

4

King of the Hill

Teams fight for control of a central objective and try to hold it under pressure.

5

Domination

Players compete for control over multiple zones, creating strategy and field movement.

6

Relay Run

A timed team objective that adds handoffs, pace, and coordination to the session.

FAQ

WaterTag questions people ask

Is WaterTag the same as a water fight?

No. A water fight is usually casual and unstructured. WaterTag turns the concept into an organized game with teams, objectives, vests, refill barrels, and scoring logic.

Can WaterTag work for large groups?

Yes. The concept is built around group play, short rounds, team formats, and rotating sessions, which makes it useful for camps, resorts, schools, and event operators.

What makes WaterTag different from laser tag or paintball?

WaterTag is wet, outdoor-friendly, easy to understand, and less aggressive than paintball. It also has a more physical summer-event feel than many laser tag setups.

You can visit the official WaterTag website at watertag.com.
